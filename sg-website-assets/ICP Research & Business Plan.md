# PART III -- ICP RESEARCH & STREAMLINED PROFILE

## 3.1 Market Landscape

The market conditions creating demand for StoryGrid's services:

**Content saturation is at an all-time high.** AI tools have made content production trivially easy, which means the average B2B SaaS company is drowning in generic, undifferentiated content. The market is "penetrated with AI-first startups" (Aneesh's own words), and with increasing competition, "how they are going to stand out is only by setting the narrative and tone right."

**Founder-led growth is now table stakes.** 79% of B2B decision-makers are more likely to support proposals from vendors that consistently produce thought leadership. Traditional brand messaging is losing trust -- decision-makers look to people, not logos, to validate business relationships. LinkedIn data shows ads from real executives outperform company page ads 3-6x in click-through rate.

**The content agency market is bifurcating.** On one end: cheap AI-generated content mills producing commodity output. On the other: premium narrative strategy firms that treat content as a business system. The middle -- generic content agencies -- is being squeezed from both sides.

**LinkedIn dominates B2B pipeline.** LinkedIn generates 80% of all B2B social media leads. Website-generated leads (often driven by LinkedIn traffic) convert at 31.3% -- more than twice the overall average. For a bootstrapped founder-agency, LinkedIn is the highest-ROI channel by a wide margin.

**Closing B2B SaaS deals requires more touchpoints than ever.** The average B2B SaaS deal in 2024 required navigating 266 touchpoints and nearly 2,900 impressions. Enterprise contracts over $100K required 417 touchpoints. This means content systems, not content pieces, drive deals.

- **Weaknesses:** SEO/blog focused, not founder narrative; no personal brand component; traditional agency model; slow
- **Pricing:** Estimated $5K-$15K/month

- **Positioning:** Write LinkedIn posts for executives; typically solo operators
- **Strengths:** Low cost ($1K-$3K/month); fast turnaround; personal attention
- **Weaknesses:** No strategy layer; no narrative architecture; no systems thinking; quality varies wildly; no AI augmentation; scale ceiling
- **Pricing:** $1K-$5K/month for 8-16 posts
- **StoryGrid's edge:** Strategy-first (not just writing); founder voice extraction methodology; AI-augmented quality and consistency; compound story system

### 5. Omniscient Digital
- **Positioning:** Content and SEO strategy for B2B SaaS
- **Strengths:** Data-driven; strong case studies; respected in SaaS
- **Weaknesses:** SEO-centric; not founder-narrative focused; expensive for early stage
- **Pricing:** $8K-$20K/month
- **StoryGrid's edge:** Founder narrative focus; accessible pricing for Series A stage; AI-first production model

## 4.3 StoryGrid's Moat

**1. Practitioner credibility.** Aneesh has $5M+ in closed B2B revenue. He is not a marketer telling founders how to sell -- he is a sales professional who understands narrative as a sales tool. This is rare and impossible to fake.

**2. AI-first production model.** AI augmentation is embedded in the methodology, not bolted on. This delivers 2-3x the output speed of traditional agencies at equivalent or higher quality, because the AI handles research, structuring, and drafting while the human layer handles strategy, voice, and refinement.

**3. Narrative Architecture as a product.** Most competitors skip strategy and go straight to execution. StoryGrid's Tier 01 (Narrative Architecture) is a standalone product that creates the foundation everything else builds on. **4. Systems thinking.** The framework (Input, Audit, Architecture, System, Execution, Compound) is a branded methodology. It communicates that content is not random acts of posting but a compounding system. This resonates with analytically-minded SaaS founders.

**5. Dual-brand architecture.** Aneesh's personal brand ("AI x Sales x Storytelling") feeds leads to StoryGrid. StoryGrid's client results feed credibility back to Aneesh. Most agency founders keep these separate or do not invest in personal brand. The flywheel is the moat.

# PART V -- 90-180 DAY GROWTH BUSINESS PLAN

## 5.1 Guiding Principles

1. **Bootstrapped reality.** No external funding. Every dollar spent must be earned or already available. Growth is funded by revenue, not runway.
2. **Founder-time is the scarcest resource.** Aneesh is the strategist, the salesperson, the delivery team, and the brand. Every activity must pass the "does this directly lead to revenue or compounding reputation?" test.
3. **LinkedIn-first.** LinkedIn is where the ICP lives, where trust is built, and where deals start. It is the primary channel for at least the first 180 days.
4. **Proof before scale.** The first 90 days are about getting 3-5 paying clients and producing undeniable results. The next 90 are about systemizing delivery and building referral loops.
5. **Price for value, not volume.** StoryGrid is a strategy firm, not a content mill. Pricing reflects the business impact of narrative, not the word count of deliverables.

    |
    v

    |
    v

**Key sales principles:**
- Never pitch on the first DM. Build rapport first (3-5 touchpoints minimum)
- The discovery call sells the thinking, not the service. Demonstrate insight during the call itself
- Proposals sent within 24 hours of the call (momentum matters)
- 3-month minimum on retainers prevents churn from clients who expect results in Week 1
- Overdeliver in Month 1. The first month's output sets the retention trajectory

## C. Discovery Call Framework

**Duration:** 25-30 minutes
**Structure:**

### Opening (2 minutes)
- Thank them for their time
- Set the agenda: "I'd like to understand where [Company] is with narrative and positioning, share a few observations, and if there's a fit, discuss how we might work together. Sound good?"
- Confirm time: "We've got 30 minutes -- I'll keep us on track."

### Discovery Questions (12 minutes)
- "Tell me about [Company]. What's the one-sentence version?"
- "What's working well right now in terms of market awareness and pipeline?"
- "Where are the gaps? If you could fix one thing about how the market perceives [Company], what would it be?"
- "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
- "What does success look like in 90 days if we got this right?"

### Insight Delivery (8 minutes)
- Share 2-3 specific observations about their current narrative, LinkedIn, or positioning (prepared before the call)
- Connect observations to the Narrative Architecture framework
- "Here's what I see: you have [strength], but the market doesn't know because [gap]. The fix is [framework concept]."

### Fit Assessment (5 minutes)
- "Based on what we've discussed, I think there's a strong fit. Here's what working together would look like: [brief description of recommended tier]."
- "The investment is [$X/month] with a [3-month] commitment. I'll send a one-page proposal within 24 hours."
- "What questions do you have?"

### Close (3 minutes)
- Confirm next step: "I'll send the proposal by [tomorrow]. Let's schedule a 15-minute follow-up for [specific date] to discuss."
- "In the meantime, is there anything else you need from me to make this decision?"
